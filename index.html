Key Characteristics of a Metroidvania

Map Opens Up With Time

Metroidvania games turn the game world into a map, which has blocked passageways and doors that require keys or other tools. The player must hunt down those objects, which then expand the map and allow the gamer to progress further.

Game is All About Exploration

Metroidvania games require the gamer to hunt for an object and use that item to move further in the game. This requires searching and traveling in areas that normally the player wouldn’t go, and makes the game feel like it has more depth.

Story and Level Design Work Together

Because every metroidvania game is a puzzle, a cohesive story is required to entice the player to continue forward. This makes the experience more immersive than a traditional side-scroller, but less so than a modern open world game.

Metroidvania

The term “metroidvania” is a portmanteau of the two video games that defined the genre: Metroid (1986) and Castlevania (1986). Future Of

Metroidvania

With the appetite among gamers for titles which offer a challenge, along with the rise of nostalgia-fuelled retro-styled games, the Metroidvania genre is not likely to be fading anytime soon. The indie game market, seeing unprecedented popularity, is all too happy to cater to this niche.
